## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most rapidly growing forms of cybercrime in India. It involves tricking users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive financial information through deceptive methods.

A typical UPI fraud begins with the attacker contacting the victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, often posing as a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer/seller. The attacker builds urgency or trust and then sends a payment request or malicious link. The victim is instructed to “approve” the request or enter their UPI PIN under the false assumption that they are receiving money. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a debit transaction, leading to immediate financial loss.

College students and young adults are frequent targets due to their high usage of digital payment platforms and tendency to engage in online transactions such as marketplace purchases or peer payments. Limited awareness of fraud tactics further increases their vulnerability.

The consequences of such fraud include direct financial loss, compromised personal data, and emotional distress. In some cases, victims may also face difficulties in recovering lost funds due to delayed reporting or lack of evidence.

To prevent UPI fraud, users must avoid sharing OTPs or PINs, verify requests before approval, and remain cautious of unsolicited communications. Awareness and responsible digital behavior are key to minimizing such cyber threats.
